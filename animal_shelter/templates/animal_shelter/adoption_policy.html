Why Adoption?

You are saving a life by adopting a pet and making a life-changing impact on the animal's life by directly contributing to reduce the biggest global problem of animal homelessness. It is a very common misconception that stray animals are dangerous and have behavioral issues. Many strays were once pets and are already habitual to living with humans. Others have developed incredible adaptability from living on the streets. With appropriate care and love, they can easily transition into living as loved family pets.

We as a Team are focused on helping stray animals in receiving the care and support they need. You can help us make a difference in the lives of animals by adopting a pet today! By choosing to adopt, not only do you bring joy into your life, but you also give a deserving animal a second chance at life.

Do's and Dont's

  • Research the Pet: Take the time to learn about different pet breeds, species, and their needs. Consider your lifestyle, living situation, and how much time you can dedicate to your pet. Different animals have different requirements.
  • Visit Local Shelters: Visit local animal shelters and get to know the animals in person. Spending time with a potential pet can help you gauge your compatibility.
  • Consider Older Pets: Older animals are often overlooked but make wonderful companions. They are usually calmer and require less training than puppies or kittens.
  • Prepare Your Home: Pet-proof your home by removing potential hazards and creating a safe, comfortable space for your new pet. Ensure your pet's living environment suits their specific needs.
  • Commitment: Understand that pet adoption is a long-term commitment. Pets can live for many years, so be ready for the responsibility.
  • Regular Veterinary Care: Make sure you can provide regular veterinary care, including vaccinations, check-ups, and addressing any health issues that may arise.
  • Training and Socialization: Be willing to invest time in training and socializing your pet, especially if they are young or have behavioral issues. Proper training is crucial for a well-adjusted pet.
  • Impulse Adoption: Don't adopt a pet on impulse. Take the time to think about the commitment and responsibilities involved.
  • Choosing Based on Looks Alone: Avoid adopting a pet solely based on their appearance. Personality, energy level, and needs should also be considered.
  • Supporting Pet Mills or Backyard Breeders: Do not buy pets from pet stores, puppy mills, or backyard breeders. These sources often prioritize profit over animal welfare.
  • Ignoring Allergies or Housing Restrictions: If you or someone in your household has allergies or if your living situation doesn't permit pets, don't ignore these factors. Ensure everyone's well-being.
  • Isolating Your Pet: Avoid leaving your pet alone for extended periods without proper care and companionship.
  • Overlooking Legal Requirements: Familiarize yourself with local laws and regulations regarding pet ownership, such as licensing and leash laws.
  • Skipping Spaying/Neutering: Do not skip spaying or neutering your pet. This is crucial for population control and can also have health and behavioral benefits.
